# CAMP HOUSE OF AI™ — THE FIRST MONTH OF MORNING LETTERS
*Skills № 002–030, written in the Camp voice and ready for the sender. Each letter: the five-year-old version, the CEO version, the five-minute drill, the Lodge line. № 030 lands on the bridge to the private engagements.*

---

**№ 002 · COPY — The Two-Sentence Brief**
*Five-year-old:* Before you ask for help, say who it's for and what "done" looks like.
*CEO:* Output quality is set before you press enter. Two sentences — audience and definition of done — eliminate 80% of editing. You're not prompting; you're briefing.
*Drill:* Before your next AI request, write: (1) who this is for, (2) what "done beautifully" looks like. Compare against your usual one-liner.
*Lodge:* Post your before-and-after — the gasps are the point.

---

**№ 003 · MINDSET — Give Her a Name and a Job**
*Five-year-old:* Helpers work better when they know what their job is.
*CEO:* An unnamed chatbot gets treated like a search bar. A named role — "you are my senior copywriter" — activates a consistent standard, a lens, and accountability. Staff, not software.
*Drill:* Open a fresh chat: "You are my [role]. You've done this for premium brands for 20 years. Your standard is [X]." Then give it today's task.
*Lodge:* Tell us what you named yours.

---

**№ 004 · TECH MAGIC — Bottle Your Voice**
*Five-year-old:* Show it three things you wrote, and it learns to sound like you.
*CEO:* Your voice is your most defensible asset. Paste three pieces you're proud of and ask AI to describe your voice in ten adjectives and three rules. Save that paragraph — it's your voice bottle. Pour it into any future chat.
*Drill:* Paste 3 favorite emails/posts → "Describe my writing voice: 10 adjectives, 3 rules, 3 phrases I overuse." Save the answer somewhere permanent.
*Lodge:* Share your favorite adjective it found.

---

**№ 005 · BUSINESS — The Follow-Up That Writes Itself**
*Five-year-old:* Tell it what happened in the meeting, and it writes the letter after.
*CEO:* Follow-ups die because they take activation energy at your lowest-energy moment. Messy notes in, polished recap out: decisions, next steps, one clear ask. Sixty seconds, sent same-day, deals move.
*Drill:* After today's next call, paste raw notes: "Warm follow-up in my voice: 3 decisions, next steps with owners, one question at the end."
*Lodge:* Post how long it took. (Members report under two minutes.)

---

**№ 006 · CLAUDE — The Filing Cabinet Trick**
*Five-year-old:* Put your important papers in one drawer, and Claude remembers them every time.
*CEO:* A Claude Project with your brand docs — offers, voice bottle, FAQ, pricing — means never re-explaining your business. Every conversation starts already knowing you. This is the difference between a temp and a tenured employee.
*Drill:* Create a Project called "[Your Business] HQ." Upload 3–5 core docs. Ask it: "What do you now know about my business?" Marvel.
*Lodge:* What surprised you about its answer?

---

**№ 007 · AI MANNERS — Thank It, Then Ask for Better**
*Five-year-old:* Say "good job — now can you make it even better?"
*CEO:* First drafts are the floor, not the ceiling. The pros iterate: "Good. Now warmer." "Now half the length." "Now like it's written on a porch, not in an office." Three rounds beats one perfect prompt every time.
*Drill:* Take any AI draft today and push it three rounds with one small instruction each. Watch round three embarrass round one.
*Lodge:* Post your favorite round-three line.

---

**№ 008 · MINDSET — The Morning Question**
*Five-year-old:* Ask, "if I could only do one thing today, what should it be?"
*CEO:* Overwhelm is a prioritization failure, not a workload failure. Paste your to-do list and yesterday's loose ends: "You're my chief of staff. If I can only do three things, which three, in what order, and why?" Then obey.
*Drill:* Do it right now with today's real list. Notice what it deprioritized that you would have done first.
*Lodge:* What did it tell you to skip?

---

**№ 009 · TECH MAGIC — Order From the Menu**
*Five-year-old:* Ask for five choices before you pick one.
*CEO:* Asking for one answer gets you the average. Asking for five ranked options — with a one-line case for each — gets you the edges, where the good ideas live. Choose like an owner, don't accept like an order-taker.
*Drill:* Next decision today: "Give me 5 genuinely different options, ranked, one sentence on why each could win."
*Lodge:* Did you pick № 1 — or the wildcard?

**№ 010 · COPY — Steal Your Own Applause**
*Five-year-old:* Your happy customers already wrote your ads. Go find them.
*CEO:* The most persuasive copy in your business is sitting in your DMs and testimonials. Paste every kind word you can find: "Extract the exact phrases clients use to describe the result. Rank by emotional punch." That's your sales page, verbatim.
*Drill:* Ten minutes of praise-mining. Paste everything. Extract the top 10 phrases.
*Lodge:* Post the single best phrase a client ever gave you.

**№ 011 · BUSINESS — The Meeting You Didn't Attend**
*Five-year-old:* It can read the long boring thing and tell you the good parts.
*CEO:* Transcripts, threads, 40-page PDFs — none of them deserve your reading time. They deserve a summary with stakes: "Decisions made, money mentioned, risks raised, what needs me personally."
*Drill:* Feed it the longest unread thing in your life right now with that exact prompt.
*Lodge:* How many pages did you just *not* read?

**№ 012 · CLAUDE — Hire the Devil's Advocate**
*Five-year-old:* Ask it to tell you why your idea might not work — before you find out the hard way.
*CEO:* Your excitement is a blind spot. Before launching anything: "Be the most skeptical, litigious, unimpressed version of my ideal client. Attack this offer. Then tell me the three fixes that would win you over." Red-team now or refund later.
*Drill:* Run your current offer or next launch through that prompt today.
*Lodge:* Share the objection that stung — and the fix.

---

**№ 013 · CHATGPT — Twenty, Then Three**
*Five-year-old:* Ask for twenty ideas fast, then keep the best three.
*CEO:* Volume is where originality hides. The first ten ideas are everyone's ideas; brilliance starts around fourteen. Demand twenty, forbid repeats, then shortlist three and develop only those.
*Drill:* "20 content ideas for [your topic]. No two alike. Number them." Circle three. Delete the rest without guilt.
*Lodge:* Post idea № 17. It's always № 17.

---

**№ 014 · AI MANNERS — Make It Interview You First**
*Five-year-old:* Tell it to ask you questions before it starts.
*CEO:* The single highest-leverage line in prompting: "Before you write anything, ask me 5 questions, one at a time, to close every gap. Don't draft until you have what you need." You stop getting generic work the moment it stops guessing.
*Drill:* Add that line to any meaningful task today. Answer honestly. Compare the result to what you usually get.
*Lodge:* Which of its questions made you actually think?

---

**№ 015 · MINDSET — The Read-Back**
*Five-year-old:* Ask, "tell me what I just asked for, in your own words."
*CEO:* Misfires happen at the briefing, not the execution. "Restate my request in your own words and list your assumptions before starting." Ten seconds of read-back saves thirty minutes of wrong work — same rule as any great kitchen.
*Drill:* Use the read-back on today's most important request. Correct one assumption before it drafts.
*Lodge:* What wrong assumption did you catch?

---

**№ 016 · TECH MAGIC — The Template Forge**
*Five-year-old:* When it makes something great, turn it into a cookie cutter.
*CEO:* Never let a great output be a one-off. "Turn this into a reusable template with [brackets] for the parts that change, plus a one-line instruction for filling it." One good Tuesday becomes every Tuesday.
*Drill:* Take the best thing AI made you this week. Forge it into a template. File it in your Project.
*Lodge:* Name the template you just forged.

---

**№ 017 · COPY — Subject Lines by the Dozen**
*Five-year-old:* Ask for twelve titles and pick the one that makes you curious.
*CEO:* The email lives or dies in the subject line, and your first instinct is usually the politest — which is the problem. Twelve options across four styles: curiosity, benefit, story, pattern-break. Pick with your gut, not your manners.
*Drill:* Next email: "12 subject lines — 3 curiosity, 3 benefit, 3 story, 3 unexpected. Under 6 words each."
*Lodge:* Post the winner and the style it came from.

**№ 018 · BUSINESS — Rehearse the Hard Conversation**
*Five-year-old:* Practice the tricky talk with a pretend person first.
*CEO:* Price objections, scope creep, the awkward renewal — rehearsed conversations go your way. "Play a warm but hesitant client who says it's too expensive. Push back realistically. After each of my replies, coach me." Sales calls stop being scary when you've already had them.
*Drill:* Five minutes of role-play on the conversation you've been avoiding.
*Lodge:* One line from your rehearsal you'll use for real.

**№ 019 · CLAUDE — The Fine-Print Reader**
*Five-year-old:* It reads the scary paper and tells you what it really says.
*CEO:* Contracts, platform policies, terms of service — paste them in: "Plain English summary, then flag anything unusual, one-sided, or risky for someone in my position, ranked by concern." Not legal advice — but a brilliant first read before you pay for one.
*Drill:* Feed it one agreement you signed without fully reading. Breathe.
*Lodge:* Did it find anything you'd missed?

**№ 020 · CHATGPT — Set It and Forget It**
*Five-year-old:* Tell it who you are once, so you never have to say it again.
*CEO:* Custom Instructions are the difference between a stranger and staff. Fill in: who you are, who you serve, your voice rules, your pet peeves ("never say 'unlock' or 'dive in'"). Every future chat starts pre-briefed.
*Drill:* Open Settings → Custom Instructions. Spend eight minutes. Include three banned words.
*Lodge:* Post your funniest banned word.

**№ 021 · AI MANNERS — The Kind No**
*Five-year-old:* It can help you say "no thank you" in a way that keeps friends.
*CEO:* Every yes you don't mean costs your calendar and your resentment budget. "Draft a warm, firm decline: honor the relationship, close the door on this ask, leave one door open on my terms." Boundaries, beautifully worded, on demand.
*Drill:* Draft the no you've owed someone for two weeks. Send it today.
*Lodge:* How did it feel to finally send it?

**№ 022 · MINDSET — One Task, Three Sizes**
*Five-year-old:* Ask for the small way, the medium way, and the fancy way.
*CEO:* Perfectionism disguises itself as diligence. "Give me the 10-minute version, the 1-hour version, and the flagship version of this task — and tell me which the situation actually deserves." Most things deserve the 10-minute version. Ship it.
*Drill:* Run today's most dreaded task through three sizes. Do the one it recommends.
*Lodge:* Which size did the task actually deserve?

**№ 023 · TECH MAGIC — The Photograph Trick**
*Five-year-old:* Take a picture of anything, and it can help you with it.
*CEO:* Your camera is an input device. Whiteboard scrawl → typed plan. Handwritten journal → organized notes. A screenshot of a confusing dashboard → plain-English explanation. If your eyes can see it, your AI can work with it.
*Drill:* Photograph one analog thing today — notes, a form, the back of a package — and ask AI to do something useful with it.
*Lodge:* Post the most surprising thing you've turned from paper to power.

**№ 026 · CLAUDE — The Editor Who Keeps Your Soul**
*Five-year-old:* Ask it to fix your writing without changing how you sound.
*CEO:* The command is "edit, don't rewrite": "Preserve my voice and structure. Fix only clarity, rhythm, and grammar. Show changes in brackets so I can accept or reject." You stay the author. It stays the red pen.
*Drill:* Run something you wrote entirely yourself through edit-don't-rewrite mode.
*Lodge:* Accept or reject — what did you overrule it on?

**№ 027 · CHATGPT — Think Out Loud on the Dock**
*Five-year-old:* You can just *talk* to it while you walk, and it takes notes.
*CEO:* Your best thinking doesn't happen at a keyboard. Voice mode + a twenty-minute walk = a rambling monologue that comes back as a structured memo: themes, decisions, next steps. Walk the dock; return with a strategy document.
*Drill:* One voice-mode walk today. End with: "Now organize everything I said into a memo with action items."
*Lodge:* Post one idea that only showed up because you were walking.

**№ 028 · AI MANNERS — Demand the Confidence Score**
*Five-year-old:* Ask, "how sure are you?" — and it will tell you the truth.
*CEO:* AI sounds equally confident when it's right and when it's guessing. The fix: "Rate your confidence 1–10 on this answer, and flag anything you assumed or couldn't verify." One line turns a smooth talker into a trustworthy advisor.
*Drill:* Add the confidence line to today's most important question. Investigate anything under an 8.
*Lodge:* What did it confess to guessing?

**№ 029 · MINDSET — Fire One Task Friday**
*Five-year-old:* Every week, pick one chore and give it away forever.
*CEO:* Firing yourself as the bottleneck isn't one grand gesture — it's a weekly ritual. Each Friday, choose one recurring task, build its template and prompt, test it twice, and never do it manually again. Fifty-two Fridays is a different life.
*Drill:* Fire your first task today. Recurring, annoying, rules-based — that's the profile. Build the prompt, run it, retire.
*Lodge:* Announce what you fired. We celebrate terminations here.

**№ 030 · BUSINESS — The Handoff Letter**
*Five-year-old:* Write down how you do your job, so helpers can do it for you.
*CEO:* Here is the graduation truth: every skill this month still requires *you* at the keyboard. The next level is systems that run without you. Start by dictating one task to AI as if training a new hire — every step, every judgment call, every "it depends." Ask it to write the SOP. That document is the seed of your infrastructure: the thing agents are built from.
*Drill:* Pick the task you fired on № 029. Talk through exactly how you do it. "Turn this into a complete SOP a capable assistant — human or AI — could follow without me."
*Lodge:* Post your SOP's title. And if writing it made you wonder *who builds the agents that run these* — that, dear camper, is a porch conversation. The House builds them. Ask us about the private engagements.
